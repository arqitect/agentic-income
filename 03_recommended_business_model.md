# Recommended Business Model

## Chosen Model

`Local Lead + Follow-Up System`

Target: small home-service and property-service businesses in Santa Cruz County.

Treat this as a working hypothesis, not a settled truth. The first `20` conversations decide whether this stays the model.

The offer is not "AI consulting." It is a practical system that helps a local operator:

- capture more quote requests
- respond faster
- stop losing leads in calls, texts, and email
- get more reviews consistently
- understand which leads turned into jobs

## Ideal Customer Profile

- 1 to 10 employee home or property service business
- owner-led or manager-led
- already doing real jobs
- has at least a basic website or Google Business Profile
- relies on inbound calls, texts, forms, or referrals
- no formal CRM or only a messy spreadsheet
- feels busy but knows follow-up is inconsistent

Best first ICP examples:

- window cleaner
- pressure washer
- gutter cleaner
- handyman
- small landscaper
- solar panel cleaner

## Problem Statement

Many local operators do not have a demand problem first. They have a systems problem:

- website visitors do not get pushed into a clear quote path
- mobile contact flows are weak
- quote requests go to email, text, voicemail, and paper notes
- no one reliably follows up after 1 day, 3 days, or 7 days
- review requests happen randomly
- no one can see which channels actually produce jobs

## Offer Ladder

### Entry offer

`Short audit or paid mini-audit`

Purpose:

- start a conversation
- show Eric understands the business
- identify obvious missed revenue points

Rule:

- if free, cap it at roughly `15 minutes` of review and one short page
- do not build custom recommendations for silent leads

### Starter offer

`Quote Flow Fix`

Includes:

- one-page audit
- improved quote form
- simple lead tracker
- follow-up reminder system
- review request template pack

Delivery constraint:

- the first version should be fulfillable in roughly `4 to 6 hours`, not a mini agency project

### Normal offer

`Local Quote System`

Includes:

- landing page or service page
- structured quote form
- CRM sheet or Airtable/Baserow base
- lead-stage tracking
- follow-up workflow
- review-request workflow
- handoff video

### Monthly retainer

`Light support and optimization`

Includes:

- monthly workflow check
- lead tracker cleanup
- minor copy or form edits
- reporting summary
- additional reminder logic as needed

### Optional performance / referral model

Use cautiously and only after trust:

- base setup fee plus small monthly
- optional referral bonus for referred clients
- optional success bonus tied to agreed operational milestones, not vague revenue promises

Do not start with pay-per-lead promises. That creates tracking fights and overexposure early.

## Starter Offer Recommendation

Best starter offer:

`5-Day Quote Flow Fix for Local Service Businesses`

Why this one:

- easy to explain
- easy to audit
- valuable without requiring ad spend
- small enough to close quickly
- can be fulfilled mostly with existing tools

## Suggested Pricing Shape

- beta: `$300 to $500`
- normal starter: `$700 to $1,300`
- monthly support: `$99 to $249`

## What To Sell In Month 1

Sell only:

- `Quote Flow Fix`
- `Local Quote System`

Do not lead month 1 with:

- property-manager operations systems
- multi-page local SEO packs
- anything that requires heavy client training or deep data handling

## What Not To Sell

- autonomous outreach bots
- "AI receptionist" hype as the main pitch
- custom software builds before demand is proven
- broad SEO retainers with vague deliverables
- large CRM migrations
- paid ads management before you have a strong intake flow
- guaranteed rankings, guaranteed leads, or guaranteed revenue

## Positioning Without Sounding Like An AI Bro Agency

Use outcome language:

- "I help local service businesses tighten their quote flow."
- "I clean up the gap between website visit and booked job."
- "I build simple systems so good leads do not get lost."
- "I can set up a lightweight follow-up process without making your team learn a huge tool."

Do not lead with:

- agents
- automations
- frontier models
- prompt engineering
- "10x growth"

If asked how the work gets done, explain that Eric uses private tools behind the scenes to research, draft, and organize, but the client is buying the result.

## When To Pivot

Pivot if any of these happen after disciplined effort:

- 20 real conversations produce no willingness to pay for the starter offer
- 5 audits do not create at least 2 strong sales conversations
- the first niche says yes to audits but no to payment
- fulfillment consistently takes too long for the price point
- another niche clearly responds faster with less friction

Likely next pivot order:

1. property management support
2. vacation rental operations support
3. selected ADU/remodel contractors
