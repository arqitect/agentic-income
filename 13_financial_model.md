# Financial Model

This model is intentionally conservative. It assumes Eric already has major infrastructure available through his homelab and is minimizing new software spend.

## Expected Costs

| Cost Item | Likely Monthly Range | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| LLM / API usage | $40 to $150 | depends on audit volume and proposal drafting |
| Domain / email overhead | $10 to $25 | if not already covered elsewhere |
| Airtable / Sheets / Baserow | $0 to $24 | free tiers first |
| n8n / server incremental cost | $0 to $30 | mostly sunk cost if homelab already running |
| Backup / storage / misc tooling | $0 to $20 | exports, logs, backups |
| Optional call-tracking later | $0 to $30 | skip at first |

Estimated starting monthly out-of-pocket:

- lean mode: `$50 to $90`
- active mode: `$100 to $180`

## Domain / Hosting Assumptions

- homelab infrastructure is mostly already paid for
- no new public SaaS stack should be added before a paid beta
- use simple hosted frontier models when quality matters

## Pricing Assumptions

- first paid beta: `$300 to $500`
- normal starter setup: `$700 to $1,300`
- larger ops setup: `$900 to $2,200`
- monthly support: `$99 to $249`

## 30 / 60 / 90-Day Revenue Scenarios

Figures below are cumulative revenue, not monthly run rate.

| Scenario | Day 30 | Day 60 | Day 90 | Assumption |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Conservative | $0 | $900 | $2,100 | no client by day 30, then 1 beta and 1 small setup |
| Base | $450 | $1,800 | $3,600 | 1 beta, then 1 to 2 standard setups and 1 support retainer |
| Aggressive | $900 | $3,000 | $5,400 | fast close on one beta, then steady closes and 2 support retainers |

Important:

- day 30 can honestly be `$0`
- the plan only works if Eric closes a paid beta before sinking too much time into automation
- treat the aggressive case as a stretch case, not the operating plan

## Break-Even Point

Operational break-even is very low because existing infrastructure reduces fixed costs.

Practical break-even:

- one paid beta likely covers a lean first month
- two small clients usually cover tool spend plus some paid time

True success threshold is not break-even. It is whether Eric's hourly return becomes worth continuing.

## Time Requirements

### Pre-client phase

- 6 to 12 hours per week

### One to two active clients

- 10 to 18 hours per week

### Three to five active clients

- 15 to 25 hours per week

If client work starts regularly exceeding the price point, either raise prices or narrow scope.

## Cash Collection Plan

- beta projects under `$600`: collect `100% upfront`
- standard setups: collect `50% upfront`, `50% on delivery`
- monthly support: bill in advance
- no work starts without payment confirmation

## Payment Terms

- proposals valid for 7 to 14 days
- payment due on receipt unless otherwise agreed
- paused projects due to missing client inputs may have timelines extended

## Refund / Guarantee Boundaries

- do not offer revenue guarantees
- do not guarantee rankings
- offer reasonable fix commitments for delivery defects
- if Eric fails to deliver agreed scope, refund should be proportional to incomplete work, not open-ended

## Operating Interpretation

The business does not need a huge number of clients to become useful income. It needs:

- one paid beta
- one repeatable offer
- one workflow that does not eat all available time

That is the threshold to protect first.
