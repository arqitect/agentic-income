# Operations Plan

## Lead Handling

1. Capture each lead in one CRM only.
2. Record source URL and why the lead looks promising.
3. Score the lead before outreach.
4. Only create a custom audit for high-potential leads.
5. Move the lead through explicit statuses:
   `new -> researched -> qualified -> audit ready -> outreach draft ready -> contacted -> replied -> call booked -> proposal sent -> won / lost`

## Client Onboarding Flow

1. Discovery conversation
2. Fit decision
3. Short scope recap in writing
4. Proposal
5. Deposit or first payment
6. Intake form / credentials collection
7. Delivery window starts
8. Review and approval
9. Handoff
10. Offer monthly support if useful

## Delivery Checklist

- confirm scope in writing
- define one primary business goal
- gather access and assets
- map current intake flow
- build the simplest useful version
- test on desktop and mobile
- document changes
- hand off with plain-language instructions

## Revision Policy

- one round of minor revisions is included in beta work
- new pages, new workflows, or strategy changes are out of scope
- scope changes must be priced before work starts

## Support Boundaries

- support means fixing what was delivered and making small agreed adjustments
- support does not mean unlimited custom requests
- support should be bounded by response window and task type

Suggested boundary:

- email support within 2 business days
- up to 30 to 60 minutes of minor edits per month on low-tier support
- anything larger becomes a scoped add-on

## Weekly Cadence

### Monday

- review pipeline
- choose this week's top leads
- schedule outreach

### Tuesday

- research and audits
- proposal work

### Wednesday

- outreach and follow-up
- fulfillment block

### Thursday

- client delivery work
- systems cleanup

### Friday

- KPI review
- invoicing
- next-week planning

## How To Avoid Scope Creep

- use a written scope before touching the work
- define exactly how many pages, forms, workflows, and revisions are included
- keep phase 1 small
- say "that can be phase 2" often
- tie every extra request back to price, time, or business goal

## File / Folder Organization

Recommended client structure outside this repo:

`/clients/client-name/`

- `01_intake/`
- `02_audit/`
- `03_proposal/`
- `04_assets/`
- `05_delivery/`
- `06_workflows/`
- `07_reporting/`
- `99_archive/`

Never store raw passwords in the repo. Use a separate secure method.

## Client Communication Rules

- be clear, short, and specific
- confirm scope in writing
- do not promise outcomes you cannot control
- do not talk in tool jargon unless asked
- document approvals before changing live assets
- do not cold-text prospects
- if using commercial cold email, include real sender identity, valid reply information, and a postal address

## When To Use AI Versus Manual Verification

Use AI for:

- research drafting
- summarizing findings
- generating first-pass audits
- writing draft outreach and proposals
- checking consistency

Manually verify:

- business facts
- pricing
- licensing and legal/compliance claims
- live links and contact details
- final outreach
- anything that will be sent to a prospect or client

## Actions That Must Always Be Human-Approved

- sending email, SMS, DMs, or voicemails
- submitting forms
- publishing pages
- changing DNS
- connecting live business tools
- spending money
- sending proposals
- accessing or sharing client credentials
