# Legal, Compliance, And Risk Checklist

## Strong Disclaimer

This is not legal, tax, or accounting advice. It is an operator checklist based on sources reviewed on `2026-05-13`. Before operating, filing, charging clients, or sending commercial outreach, verify requirements with official sources and, where needed, a qualified professional.

## Business License / Tax Certificate Considerations

### City of Santa Cruz

- The City of Santa Cruz states that doing business in city limits requires a `Business License Tax Certificate`.
- The application must be submitted `on or before the day you start business`.
- The city also lists `occasional transaction` rules for people with no fixed city location who come into the city for specific work.
- The city's published fee page showed a basic tax of `$145.15` plus employee fees, plus a `$35` registration fee and `$4` state fee for new licenses as of the accessed source date.

Practical rule:

- if Eric is based in Santa Cruz city limits, or operating there for clients, verify city requirements before invoicing or starting work

### County vs. city differences

- Santa Cruz County's business-information PDF says the county does `not` require a general business license in `unincorporated` areas such as Live Oak, Pleasure Point, Soquel, Aptos, and Felton
- that does not mean "no rules apply"
- zoning, home-occupation, environmental health, or special permits may still apply
- other cities like Capitola, Scotts Valley, and Watsonville have their own licensing requirements
- Capitola's official page says businesses conducting business in the city with projected annual gross revenues above `$3,074` need a business license, and home-based businesses may also need a home occupation permit
- Scotts Valley's official FAQ says businesses located within city limits, or doing business in Scotts Valley, need a business license; the FAQ also says home-based businesses still need one

## Fictitious Business Name Consideration

- If Eric uses a business name that is not his legal name, Santa Cruz County says a `Fictitious Business Name` statement may be required.
- The County Clerk says it should be filed within `40 days` of first transacting business under a fictitious name.
- Publication requirements may apply for a new filing.

## Basic Contract / Scope Requirement

Every paid project should have, at minimum:

- client name
- scope
- timeline
- price
- payment terms
- revision boundaries
- exclusions
- ownership / access expectations
- no-guarantee language

Do not start work based on a vague text thread.

## No Guaranteed Marketing Outcomes

Never promise:

- guaranteed leads
- guaranteed rankings
- guaranteed revenue
- guaranteed close rate

Better language:

- "I will deliver the agreed system and recommend best practices."
- "I cannot control market demand, owner response time, or platform behavior."

## Privacy And Data Handling

- store only the minimum needed data
- separate client assets from credentials
- do not commit secrets or passwords into the repo
- use a secure password manager or secure sharing method
- avoid collecting consumer personal data unless the client truly needs it
- define who can access CRM records

## Email Outreach Caution

The FTC's CAN-SPAM guidance applies to commercial email, including B2B messages.

Minimum practical rules:

- accurate `from` and reply information
- truthful subject lines
- identify the sender clearly
- include a valid postal address
- provide a working opt-out method
- honor opt-out requests promptly
- monitor anyone acting on your behalf

Because Eric is doing small-batch local outreach, the safest approach is low-volume, personalized, permission-oriented outreach with fast opt-out compliance.

## Marketing Text / SMS Caution

- do not cold text prospects
- do not auto-send review or marketing texts in version 1
- if a client wants SMS workflows, make them prove they have the right customer consent and opt-out handling
- if consent records are unclear, use manual email instead

FCC guidance treats marketing texts seriously, and text-based telemarketing carries additional TCPA risk. Do not improvise here.

## Client Credential Handling

- request only the credentials actually needed
- prefer temporary or delegated access where possible
- rotate or remove access when work ends
- document where live assets are hosted
- never store client passwords in plain text project files

## Insurance Considerations

Needs professional review, but practical topics include:

- general liability if meeting clients or handling on-site activity
- professional liability / E&O once paid projects grow
- cyber/privacy risk if storing client lead data

Do not assume student/small size removes risk.

## Contractor / Physical-Service Brokering Risk

If Eric starts brokering contractors or presenting himself as the provider of physical construction services, risk rises sharply.

Issues to verify:

- California contractor-license rules
- whether referral/broker language creates liability
- insurance coverage
- whether any compensation structure crosses into a regulated activity

CSLB says contractor licensing is required in California if work requires a building permit, uses additional workers, or total contract cost is `$1,000` or more. That does not mean Eric is a contractor now, but it is a warning not to drift into contractor representation or bidding without understanding the rules.
