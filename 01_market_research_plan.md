# Market Research Plan

## Purpose

This plan keeps Santa Cruz market claims grounded. Every claim used in outreach, proposals, pricing, or published copy should be backed by a source, dated, and marked as verified or needing verification.

## Research Questions

1. Which Santa Cruz County niches already have visible local demand and weak intake systems?
2. Which categories are easiest to sell into as a new local operator?
3. Which businesses are likely to pay a setup fee quickly?
4. Which niches have recurring follow-up or review-management value?
5. What local market factors matter most right now: tourism, housing pressure, ADUs, property management, short-term rentals, coastal upkeep?
6. Which cities and unincorporated areas should be prioritized first?
7. What licensing or compliance requirements apply to Eric's own operation?

## Practical Rule On Market Context

Tourism, ADUs, housing pressure, and short-term rentals are internal market context. They are not the opening line of outreach to a window cleaner or handyman unless the business itself clearly serves that demand.

## How To Evaluate Market Demand

Use a practical operator rubric:

1. Lead density: can you find 20 to 50 relevant businesses quickly?
2. Obvious pain: weak website, no quote form, slow/mobile-bad contact flow, low review count, no SMS-friendly intake.
3. Payability: does the business likely make enough per job to justify roughly a $300 to $1,300 starter setup?
4. Speed: can the owner say yes without a committee?
5. Delivery fit: can Eric fulfill the promised result in under 10 hours for the first version?
6. Recurring need: follow-up, review requests, new landing pages, or reporting.

## How To Avoid Hallucinated Claims

- Do not state a statistic without attaching a URL and access date.
- If the source is a tourism org, say so. Do not call it a government source if it is not.
- If a statistic is older than 12 months, mark it as possibly stale.
- If a search result snippet and the live page conflict, trust the live page.
- If a claim appears in only one source, mark `needs verification`.
- If a directory listing exists but the business website is dead, mark the listing `outdated candidate`.
- Never invent business counts, pricing, review totals, or licensing rules.

## Instructions Before Using Any Claim In Outreach Or Published Material

1. Open the original source.
2. Record the URL and access date.
3. Confirm the claim still appears on the page.
4. If the claim affects compliance, pricing, or taxes, prefer official city/county/state/federal sources.
5. If a claim cannot be confirmed, write `needs verification` and do not use it as a hard fact in outreach.
