# Santa Cruz Agentic Income Plan

This repository is a practical operating system for Eric Fadeyev to create income in Santa Cruz County by selling simple, outcome-based systems to local businesses. The business is not "AI services" in the abstract. The offer is: help local businesses capture more quote requests, follow up faster, clean up intake, ask for reviews consistently, and keep lead tracking from falling apart.

The recommended starting point is owner-operated home and property service businesses, especially exterior cleaning and adjacent property-maintenance categories such as pressure washing, window cleaning, gutter cleaning, solar panel cleaning, handyman/property maintenance, and small landscaping operators. These businesses usually have clear pain, easy-to-understand ROI, short sales cycles, and visible problems on their sites and intake flows that Eric can fix without building a giant custom system.

## Quickstart

If you only do one thing, follow `GETTING_STARTED.md` for the linear setup-to-first-client workflow.

Suggested order:

1. `GETTING_STARTED.md`
2. `00_executive_summary.md`
3. `03_recommended_business_model.md`
4. `09_lead_generation_system.md`
5. `10_sales_outreach.md`
6. `20_client_delivery_sops.md`

1. Pick one tight niche cluster first: exterior cleaning/property maintenance.
2. Research 25 local leads manually and score them.
3. Create 5 short audits for the best-fit businesses.
4. Start 10 to 20 human-approved conversations by email, phone, walk-in, or DM.
5. Sell one paid beta setup before building anything complicated.

The fastest sale is usually a one-week "quote flow + follow-up fix" rather than a full retainer. That means simple deliverables: a cleaner quote form, a basic CRM sheet, a follow-up reminder system, and a review request workflow.

## How AI Fits

AI is leverage behind the scenes, not the product pitch. Hermes, n8n, Open WebUI, and lightweight data tools help Eric research markets, prepare audits, draft outreach, assemble proposals, and keep operations organized. They do not send outreach, publish content, or contact clients without human approval.

Good positioning:

- "I help local service businesses tighten their quote flow and follow-up."
- "I clean up the stuff between 'someone looked you up' and 'you got the job.'"
- "I can set up a simple system so new leads stop getting lost."

Bad positioning:

- "I run cutting-edge autonomous AI agents for your business."
- "I will replace your staff with AI."
- "I guarantee leads or revenue."

## Recommended First Niche

Recommended first niche: owner-operated exterior cleaning and property-maintenance businesses in Santa Cruz County.

Why this niche first:

- Clear outcome: more quote requests and faster response.
- Easy to spot weak websites and missing quote flows.
- Many operators still rely on calls, texts, and manual follow-up.
- Local weather, coastal homes, rentals, and property upkeep create recurring demand.
- Sales cycles are usually shorter than restaurants, hotels, or remodel contractors.
- Eric can fulfill the first offers with existing tools and manual effort.

## Roadmap

### 7-Day Roadmap

- Confirm operating name, city/county business-license situation, and whether an FBN is needed.
- Load the CRM schema and create the first 25-lead batch.
- Produce 5 audits.
- Start the first 10 human-approved outreach attempts.
- Book 2 to 3 conversations.

### 14-Day Roadmap

- Reach 20 total conversations or contact attempts with real feedback.
- Send 3 proposals.
- Close 1 paid beta client.
- Deliver the beta manually with minimal automation.
- Document what actually took time.

### 30-Day Roadmap

- Finish 1 paid beta and collect testimonial or case-study permission.
- Close a second client or monthly support agreement.
- Standardize delivery into repeatable SOPs.
- Add n8n only where the manual process already works.

### 90-Day Roadmap

- Narrow to the best converting niche and offer.
- Maintain a stable weekly lead-generation cadence.
- Run 2 to 5 active clients with simple delivery systems.
- Track effective hourly rate and retention.
- Decide whether to stay a solo operator, raise prices, or deepen into one vertical.

## Ground Rules

- No outreach is sent automatically.
- No client contact happens without human approval.
- No public exposure of internal dashboards or secrets.
- No scraping that violates site terms or causes abusive traffic.
- No massive buildout before a paid beta proves demand.
