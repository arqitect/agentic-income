# Validation Plan

The purpose of validation is to prove someone will pay for the system before Eric overbuilds tooling.

## Targets

- `20` conversations target
- `5` audits target
- `3` proposals target
- `1` paid beta target

## What Counts As Validation

- a real prospect agrees the problem is painful
- a real prospect understands the offer quickly
- a real prospect pays for a beta setup
- delivery creates a result the client can understand and value

## What Counts As Failure

- 20 real conversations with no willingness to pay
- audits get polite interest but no movement
- proposals stall because the offer is unclear or too broad
- fulfillment time makes the price unsustainable

## Pivot Triggers

- exterior cleaning operators consistently like the audit but refuse paid setup
- property managers respond with stronger urgency and budget
- ADU/remodel firms close slower but at much healthier price points
- restaurants consume attention but do not convert

## Validation Sequence

1. 25 leads
2. 5 audits
3. 20 conversations or strong contact attempts
4. 3 proposals
5. 1 paid beta

Do not build a more complex stack until step 5 is hit or clearly within reach.

## Anti-Overbuild Check

If Eric spends more than a couple of focused hours building new automation before landing a paid beta, stop and reset. Pre-revenue, the business is validated by paid work, not by workflow completeness.
